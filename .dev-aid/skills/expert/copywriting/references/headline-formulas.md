# Headline Formulas

## 1. Number-Based Headlines

```
[Number] Ways to [Achieve Desired Outcome]
→ "7 Ways to Double Your Email Open Rates"

[Number] [Noun] That Will [Positive Outcome]
→ "5 Tools That Will Transform Your Workflow"

[Number] Reasons Why [Statement]
→ "3 Reasons Why Your Landing Page Isn't Converting"

[Number] Mistakes [Audience] Make (And How to Fix Them)
→ "9 Mistakes Startups Make When Hiring (And How to Fix Them)"

[Number] Secrets of [Successful Group]
→ "12 Secrets of 7-Figure Copywriters"

The Top [Number] [Noun] for [Audience/Goal]
→ "The Top 10 Productivity Apps for Remote Teams"
```

**Why numbers work**:
- Specific and concrete
- Set clear expectations
- Easy to scan
- Odd numbers (7, 9, 11) often outperform even

## 2. How-To Headlines

```
How to [Achieve Goal] (Without [Common Obstacle])
→ "How to Lose Weight (Without Giving Up Carbs)"

How to [Achieve Goal] in [Timeframe]
→ "How to Learn Spanish in 30 Days"

How to [Achieve Goal] Like [Expert/Celebrity]
→ "How to Negotiate Like a FBI Hostage Expert"

How [Group] [Achieve Goal]
→ "How Top Performers Start Their Morning"

How to [Achieve Goal] Even If [Objection]
→ "How to Build Muscle Even If You Have No Time"

How to Get [Desired Outcome] From [Resource]
→ "How to Get More Leads From Your Website"
```

---

## 3. Question Headlines

```
Are You Making These [Number] Mistakes?
→ "Are You Making These 5 SEO Mistakes?"

What If You Could [Desirable Outcome]?
→ "What If You Could Work 4 Hours Less Per Week?"

Do You [Common Problem/Question]?
→ "Do You Struggle With Imposter Syndrome?"

Why Do [Audience] [Action]?
→ "Why Do Smart People Make Dumb Decisions?"

Which [Option] Is Right for You?
→ "Which Project Management Tool Is Right for Your Team?"

What's the Best Way to [Achieve Goal]?
→ "What's the Best Way to Learn Coding in 2024?"
```

**Why questions work**:
- Engage readers mentally
- Create curiosity
- Readers answer in their head

```

Why [Audience] Are [Action/State]
→ "Why Developers Are Switching to TypeScript"

Why You Should [Action] (Even If [Objection])
→ "Why You Should Raise Your Prices (Even If You're Scared)"

Why [Expert/Company] [Action]
→ "Why Apple Removed the Headphone Jack"

Why [Outcome] Is [Adjective]
```

## 6. Benefit-Driven Headlines

```
Get [Benefit] Without [Pain Point]
→ "Get More Clients Without Cold Calling"

[Achieve Goal] While You [Do Something Else]
→ "Build Your Personal Brand While You Sleep"

[Verb] [Desired Outcome] in [Timeframe]
→ "Grow Your Revenue 10x in One Year"

Stop [Pain Point]. Start [Benefit].
→ "Stop Guessing. Start Knowing."

From [Bad State] to [Good State]
→ "From Overwhelmed to Organized in 7 Days"
```

## 7. Curiosity Gap Headlines

```
The [Noun] [Group] Don't Want You to Know
→ "The Strategy Big Brands Don't Want You to Know"

What [Expert/Celebrity] Knows That You Don't
→ "What Warren Buffett Knows That You Don't"

The Hidden [Noun] Behind [Topic]
→ "The Hidden Cost Behind 'Free' Software"

[Something Unexpected] About [Topic]
→ "The Surprising Truth About Motivation"

I [Did Something]. Here's What Happened.
→ "I Quit Social Media for 30 Days. Here's What Happened."
```

**Warning**: Don't be clickbait-y. Deliver on the promise.

## 8. Contrast Headlines

```
[Do This], Not [That]
→ "Build Systems, Not Goals"

Why [Conventional Wisdom] Is Wrong
→ "Why 'Follow Your Passion' Is Bad Advice"

[Group A] vs. [Group B]: [Comparison]
→ "Freelancers vs. Agencies: Which Is Right for Your Project?"

The Difference Between [A] and [B]
→ "The Difference Between Good and Great Design"

[Good Outcome] vs. [Bad Outcome]
→ "Wealth vs. Rich: What's the Difference?"
```

---

## 9. Fear/Problem Headlines

```
[Number] Warning Signs of [Problem]
→ "6 Warning Signs Your Website Is Losing Customers"

Don't [Action] Until You [Prerequisite]
→ "Don't Launch Your Startup Until You Read This"

Is [Problem] Costing You [Loss]?
→ "Is Poor UX Costing You Sales?"

The [Adjective] Mistake [Audience] Make
→ "The Biggest Mistake First-Time Managers Make"

How [Problem] Is [Negative Consequence]
→ "How Technical Debt Is Killing Your Productivity"
```

**Use sparingly**: Fear headlines work but can feel manipulative if overused.

## 10. Social Proof Headlines

```
How [Person/Company] [Achieved Outcome]
→ "How Dropbox Grew to 500M Users"

What [Number] [Audience] Taught Me About [Topic]
→ "What 100 Failed Startups Taught Me About Fundraising"

[Company/Person] Uses This [Strategy] to [Outcome]
→ "Netflix Uses This Framework to Ship Faster"

Join [Number]+ [Audience] Who [Action]
→ "Join 50,000+ Marketers Who Read This Weekly"

[Testimonial Quote]
→ "'This tool changed how we work.' - CEO, TechCorp"
```

## 11. Urgency/Scarcity Headlines

```
Last Chance: [Offer]
→ "Last Chance: 50% Off Ends Tonight"

[Time-Limited Opportunity]
→ "Only 24 Hours Left to Get Early Access"

[Number] Spots Left for [Offer]
→ "Only 10 Spots Left for Our Workshop"

Before You [Action], Read This
→ "Before You Buy a New Laptop, Read This"

[Time] to [Deadline/Event]
→ "7 Days to Launch: Are You Ready?"
```

**Use ethically**: Only use real scarcity/urgency. Fake urgency destroys trust.

```
Forget Everything You Know About [Topic]
```

## Headline Testing Checklist

Before publishing, ask:

- [ ] **Specific**: Is there a number, timeframe, or specific outcome?
- [ ] **Beneficial**: Is the reader benefit clear?
- [ ] **Urgent**: Is there a reason to read now?
- [ ] **Unique**: Is it different from competitors?
- [ ] **Scannable**: Can it be understood in 2 seconds?
- [ ] **Honest**: Does the content deliver on the promise?

---

## A/B Testing Best Practices

1. **Test one variable at a time**
   - Number vs. no number
   - Question vs. statement
   - Benefit vs. curiosity

2. **Sample sizes matter**
   - At least 1000 views per variant
   - 95% confidence level

3. **Headlines to always test**
   - Homepage hero
   - Email subject lines
   - Landing page H1s
   - Blog post titles

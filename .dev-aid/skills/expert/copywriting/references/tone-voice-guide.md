# Tone & Voice Guide

## 1. Voice vs. Tone

### Voice (Consistent)
Your brand's personality - who you are. Stays constant across all content.

**Voice characteristics**:
- Professional or casual?
- Serious or playful?
- Technical or accessible?
- Traditional or modern?

### Tone (Variable)
How you express your voice in different contexts. Adapts to situation.

**Tone adjustments**:
- Error message: Helpful, calm
- Success message: Celebratory, encouraging
- Sales page: Confident, persuasive
- Support docs: Clear, patient

---

## 2. Voice Spectrum Framework

### Formal <-----> Casual

```
Formal:
- "We appreciate your business"
- "Your request has been submitted"
- "Please contact our support team"

Casual:
- "Thanks for being here"
- "Got it! We're on it"
- "Hit us up anytime"
```

### Expert <-----> Approachable

```
Expert:
- "Leveraging ML algorithms for predictive analytics"
- "Our proprietary methodology ensures accuracy"

Approachable:
- "We use smart tech to predict what's next"
- "Our proven process just works"
```

### Serious <-----> Playful

```
Serious:
- "Security is our top priority"
- "We take your privacy seriously"

Playful:
- "Your data is locked up tighter than Fort Knox"
- "We guard your info like a dragon guards treasure"
```

## 3. Building a Voice Document

### Voice Attributes (Pick 3-5)

```markdown
## Our Voice

We are:
- **Confident** (not arrogant)
- **Clear** (not simplistic)
- **Helpful** (not condescending)
- **Human** (not robotic)
- **Direct** (not blunt)
```

### "This, Not That" Examples

```markdown
## How We Sound

✅ "Here's what you need to know"
❌ "The following information is pertinent"

✅ "Let's fix this together"
❌ "An error has occurred"

✅ "Got questions? We're here"
❌ "Submit an inquiry through our contact form"

✅ "Your trial starts now!"
❌ "Your trial period has been initiated"
```

### Vocabulary Guidelines

```markdown
## Words We Use / Don't Use

| Use This | Not This |
|----------|----------|
| Help | Assist |
| Use | Utilize |
| Start | Commence |
| About | Approximately |
| Buy | Purchase |
| Get | Obtain |
| End | Terminate |
| Now | Currently |
```

## 4. Tone by Content Type

### Marketing Copy
- **Tone**: Confident, inspiring, benefit-focused
- **Goal**: Persuade and excite
- **Example**: "Transform your workflow. Ship faster. Stress less."

### Product UI
- **Tone**: Clear, helpful, concise
- **Goal**: Guide without friction
- **Example**: "Save changes?" / "Your file is ready"

### Support/Help Docs
- **Tone**: Patient, thorough, reassuring
- **Goal**: Solve problems clearly
- **Example**: "Here's how to reset your password, step by step."

### Error Messages
- **Tone**: Calm, helpful, non-blaming
- **Goal**: Explain + provide next step
- **Example**: "We couldn't process that. Try again or contact support."

### Success Messages
- **Tone**: Positive, encouraging, brief
- **Goal**: Confirm and celebrate
- **Example**: "You're all set! Welcome aboard."

### Email Marketing
- **Tone**: Personal, conversational, valuable
- **Goal**: Build relationship + drive action
- **Example**: "Quick tip that saved us 10 hours last week..."

### Social Media
- **Tone**: Engaging, current, personality-forward
- **Goal**: Connect and interact
- **Example**: Depends heavily on platform and audience

## 5. Audience-Based Tone Adjustments

### B2B vs B2C

**B2B Tone**:
- More formal/professional
- Focus on ROI, efficiency, results
- Decision-maker language
- Industry terminology acceptable

**B2C Tone**:
- More casual/friendly
- Focus on emotions, lifestyle, identity
- Relatable, everyday language
- Avoid jargon

### Technical vs Non-Technical

**Technical Audience**:
```
"Our API supports RESTful endpoints with OAuth 2.0
authentication. Response times average 50ms."
```

**Non-Technical Audience**:
```
"Connect to any tool in minutes. No coding required.
Everything just works."
```

### New vs Existing Customers

**New Customers**:
- Educational tone
- Explain benefits clearly
- Build trust
- Reduce friction

**Existing Customers**:
- Familiar tone
- Assume knowledge
- Focus on new value
- Reward loyalty

## 6. Emotional Tone Mapping

### Match Tone to Emotional State

```markdown
User is frustrated (error/problem):
→ Calm, helpful, solution-focused
→ "We know this is frustrating. Here's how to fix it."

User is excited (new purchase/signup):
→ Enthusiastic, celebratory, forward-looking
→ "Welcome! You're going to love this."

User is uncertain (consideration stage):
→ Reassuring, educational, low-pressure
→ "Take your time. Here's what you need to know."

User is busy (in workflow):
→ Brief, clear, action-oriented
→ "Done. What's next?"
```

## 7. Writing Style Guidelines

### Sentence Structure

```markdown
✅ Short sentences. Easy to scan. Quick to understand.
❌ Long, complicated sentences that wind their way through
   multiple clauses and subclauses, making the reader work
   hard to extract the meaning.

Rule: Average 15-20 words per sentence
Mix: Some short (5-10 words), some medium (15-25 words)
```

### Paragraph Structure

```markdown
✅ One idea per paragraph
✅ 2-4 sentences maximum
✅ White space between paragraphs

❌ Wall-of-text paragraphs with multiple ideas crammed
   together that make readers' eyes glaze over
```

### Active vs Passive Voice

```markdown
✅ Active: "We ship your order within 24 hours"
❌ Passive: "Your order will be shipped within 24 hours"

✅ Active: "Click the button to save"
❌ Passive: "The button should be clicked to save"

Exception: When de-emphasizing blame
✅ "Your password was changed" (not "You changed your password")
```

### Contractions

```markdown
Casual/Friendly voice → Use contractions
- "You're" not "You are"
- "It's" not "It is"
- "Don't" not "Do not"
- "We'll" not "We will"

Formal/Professional voice → Fewer contractions
- Use full forms for emphasis: "Do not share your password"
```

## 8. Inclusive Language

### Gender-Neutral Writing

```markdown
❌ "Each user should update his password"
✅ "Each user should update their password"
✅ "Users should update their passwords"

❌ "Businessman", "Manpower", "Man-hours"
✅ "Business professional", "Workforce", "Work hours"
```

### Ability-Inclusive Language

```markdown
❌ "Simple enough for anyone"
✅ "Straightforward and accessible"

❌ "See the difference"
✅ "Notice the difference" / "Experience the difference"

❌ "Crazy good", "Insane deal"
✅ "Incredibly good", "Amazing deal"
```

### Cultural Sensitivity

```markdown
- Avoid idioms that don't translate globally
- Be careful with humor (varies by culture)
- Don't assume holidays, seasons, or cultural references
- Use inclusive examples (diverse names, scenarios)
```

## 9. Brand Personality Archetypes

### The Sage (Wisdom)
- **Voice**: Knowledgeable, trustworthy, guiding
- **Brands**: Google, BBC, Mayo Clinic
- **Copy style**: Educational, research-backed, authoritative

### The Hero (Courage)
- **Voice**: Bold, confident, empowering
- **Brands**: Nike, FedEx, BMW
- **Copy style**: Motivational, action-oriented, achievement-focused

### The Rebel (Liberation)
- **Voice**: Disruptive, provocative, challenging
- **Brands**: Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel
- **Copy style**: Bold statements, challenges conventions

### The Everyman (Belonging)
- **Voice**: Friendly, humble, relatable
- **Brands**: IKEA, Target, Budweiser
- **Copy style**: Casual, inclusive, down-to-earth

### The Magician (Transformation)
- **Voice**: Visionary, imaginative, transformative
- **Brands**: Apple, Disney, Tesla
- **Copy style**: Inspirational, possibility-focused

### The Caregiver (Service)
- **Voice**: Nurturing, supportive, reassuring
- **Brands**: Johnson & Johnson, Volvo, UNICEF
- **Copy style**: Empathetic, helpful, protective

## 10. Voice Audit Checklist

Review your copy against these questions:

- [ ] Does this sound like us?
- [ ] Would our ideal customer relate to this?
- [ ] Is it consistent with our other content?
- [ ] Does it match the context (error vs celebration)?
- [ ] Is it appropriate for the channel (email vs social)?
- [ ] Does it respect our audience's intelligence?
- [ ] Is it inclusive and accessible?
- [ ] Would we be embarrassed if this went viral?
